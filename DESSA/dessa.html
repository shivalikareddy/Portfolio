Project Aim /

Designing an interconnected system of services to facilitate the transition towards a regenerative urban economy through decentralized resource management.

01 Scanning

Theme

Regenerative Economies

An economic model that restores and renews natural, social, and economic systems by prioritizing circular flows, long-term resilience, and shared prosperity over extractive, short-term growth.

Trend Clusters

A synthesis of emerging technologies and behavioural signals identified as critical patterns for regenerative urban growth.

01 Microgrids +
Decentralized energy systems that can operate independently or in conjunction with the main power grid, enhancing localized resilience.
02 15 Minute Cities +
Urban planning models ensuring all essential resident needs are within a short walk or cycle from their homes.
03 Disaster Ready Design +
Infrastructural designs prioritizing durability and quick recovery in the face of climate-related disruptions.
04 Precision Agriculture +
Hyper-local food production optimized via IoT and data analytics for maximum resource efficiency.
05 Carbon Farming +
Agricultural practices focused on sequestering atmospheric carbon into the soil to mitigate climate change.
06 Zero Waste Packaging +
Compostable or infinitely recyclable materials that eliminate pollution in consumer delivery loops.
07 Synthetic Biology +
Engineered living systems driving innovation in circular agriculture and ecological restoration.
08 Waste Heat Markets +
Transforming industrial byproducts into tradable thermal resources for local energy efficiency.
09 Agricultural Finance +
Innovative financial models supporting regenerative practices and technological resilience in farming.
10 Food Waste Upcycling +
Repurposing surplus food into new value-added products to reduce methane emissions.

CHOSEN COMPANY: DREAM UNLIMITED

Dream Unlimited is a Canadian developer creating large-scale, master-planned communities focused on sustainability, resilience, and low-carbon urban living.

01 / MARKET POSITION

Combines real estate and sustainability through large-scale mixed-use projects.

02 / FUNDS

$28B under Asset Management

03 / DEMOGRAPHICS

Families, professionals, and retirees seeking connected and convenient communities.

04 / AUDIENCE

Investors and institutions seeking long-term returns and visible environmental impact.

05 / STRENGTHS

Delivers large-scale, low-carbon communities with strong municipal partnerships.

06 / WEAKNESSES

Sustainability-focused projects with limited self-sufficiency and tech visibility.

Generative Questions

01 /

How might we empower residents to independently manage their local energy while participating in a shared community utility?

02 /

How might we integrate emerging technologies like AI and geothermal storage to optimize energy performance and reduce costs across connected communities?

03 /

How might we create sustainable infrastructure that not only supports the community but also improves the local ecosystem?

04 /

How might Dream create a network of interconnected regenerative cities, where each neighborhood microgrid trades energy and data across a distributed regional grid?

Concept

DESSA (Dream Energy Service and System Architecture)

DESSA is not a product. It is a scaleable framework to be applied to all of Dream’s future developments. It redefines energy systems as integrated physical, digital, and social infrastructure, positioning Dream as a long-term community operator.

The Plan

01

Shifts from collaborative developer to integrated community builder and operator.

02

DESSA enables Dream to design, finance, and operate communities independently.

03

Energy, mobility, and services become recurring revenue streams.

04

Over time, DESSA scales into a licensable, adaptable urban platform.

Primary Features

Smart Mobility Network

Creates a seamless local system for clean movement and charging.

Resilience by Design

Ensures every community can operate independently when needed.

Community Energy Buy-In

Turns residents into co-owners of their local energy system.
